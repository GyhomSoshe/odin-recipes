Hachis Parmentier

Description

Another way to bake masched potatoes, this time over grinded meat.

Ingredients

  • 1kg ground beef
  • 900 g floury potatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 3 medium garlic cloves
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • a handful fresh parsley chopped
  • a handful cheese grated
  • 125 cup whole milk
  • nutmeg
  • pepper
  • salt

Steps

  1. Peel the potatoes and dice them into small bits. Add them to a large casserole, add a dash of salt and lots of water, boil them.
  2. Cook until tender and then drain them. In the meantime peel the onion and garlic. Add them to another casserole together with 2 tablespoons of butter and the bay leaves.
  3. Place it over medium heat and gently fry the onion for 5 minutes.
  4. Season with a pinch of pepper, salt and nutmeg.
  5. Stir well and then add the minced beef.
  6. Break the mince up a bit and stir well again. Put a lid on the pan and let the beef cook for about 15 to 20 minutes.
  7. Stir in the freshly chopped parsley.
  8. Take the beef off the heat and let it cool a little. In the meantime add the drained potatoes back to the casserole you cooked them in. Add the rest of the unsalted butter.
  9. Mash the potatoes up. Season with a good dash of pepper, salt and nutmeg. Pour in the whole milk.
  10. Mash them again until creamy. Then spread a little layer of the potato mash in a baking dish (this will absorb any juice coming from the cooked beef).
  11. Top with the cooked beef mince.
  12. Finish with the rest of the mashed potatoes. Sprinkle with the grated cheese. Bake the parmentier in a preheated over at 200Â°C for 20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown. Serve hot.
