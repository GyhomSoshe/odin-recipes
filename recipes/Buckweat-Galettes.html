Buckweat Galettes

Description

These pancakes are eaten all year long in France, more usually in the western regions.

Ingredients

  • 150 g buckwheat flour
  • 350 ml/g cold water
  • big pinch fine sea salt
  • 1 large egg Large
  • butter to grease the frying pan

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk the buckwheat flour and half of the water to a smooth paste.
  2. Add the egg and the remaining water and whisk again until you have a reasonably thin batter.
  3. Cover the bowl and place in the fridge to rest for (preferably) a minimum of 4 hours. This will ensure the flour is fully hydrated.
  4. Bring the batter back to room temperature before cooking.
  5. When ready to make the galettes, re-whisk the galette batter until even. Keep the whisk handy, beating again every couple of pancakes to ensure the consistency remains stable.
  6. Heat the pancake frying pan until hot. Using a thick wad of kitchen paper, carefully rub a little butter around the pan so there remains only a light oil smear on the base.
  7. Ladle or pour a little batter into the pan and quickly swirl to spread into a large thin circle. Fill in any gaps, keeping the batter thin.
  8. Cook for approximately 40 to 60 seconds until the edges come away from the pan. Do not press the galette down during cooking. Gently ease a spatula under the galette and when it can be moved freely in the pan, flip it over to cook the other side. The top of the pancake should now have a 'lacy' texture from the formation of air bubbles.
  9. Cook the other side of the pancake for a further 40 to 60 seconds (approx) until it has gently browned underneath.
  10. Remove the galette to a plate and cover with a clean tea towel.
  11. Freshly butter the base of the pan as previously (and wipe out any crumbs left behind). Then cook the next pancake as before.
  12. Continue until all the pancake batter has been used.
